# AI Cheating in Technical Assessments

[[Jordan Mazer]]'s observed trend in hiring.

## The Data
- **2 in 3 candidates** flagged for suspicious AI use in technical assessments
- **Higher rate at more prestigious universities** — counterintuitive finding
- He is sharing this as an industry challenge, not just complaining

## His Position
- Tool use IS valuable and will be vetted — just **later** in the process
- First: validate fundamental understanding WITHOUT tools
- Then: evaluate HOW candidates use tools

## The Concern
> "Mastery of fundamental technical concepts may actually be MORE important in an AI world, not less."

## His Warning to Candidates
> "If you are using tools on a test, assume that the platforms you're using have technology built in to catch you."
