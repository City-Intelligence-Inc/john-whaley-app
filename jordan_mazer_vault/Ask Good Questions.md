# Ask Good Questions

[[Jordan Mazer]] believes candidates should actively interrogate the opportunity.

## His Framing
> "Don't sell yourself short in interviews: they're lucky to have you, so act like it."

## Why It Works
- Great employers see inquisitiveness as a **positive signal**
- Less-than-great employers will dodge your questions — you've learned something
- If they don't hire you because you asked questions, was it a job worth taking?

## The Biggest Decisions in Life (his list)
1. Who you marry
2. Where you live
3. Buying a home
4. **Taking a job**

You ask plenty of questions for the other three.

## What Makes a Good Question
> "The best candidates ask questions an LLM can't answer."
> Use AI to prepare your research, then ask something that requires insider knowledge.
