# Luck + Preparation

[[Jordan Mazer]]'s nuanced view on the role of luck in careers.

## His Day-2 Story at a16z
- Week 1 at a16z, founder needed help filling a CxO role
- The candidate who rejected the offer turned out to be someone Jordan had placed 10 years earlier
- Called him, resolved concerns, candidate accepted in 2 weeks
- "Big W" on day 2 of a new job

## The Lesson
> "Luck does play a big role in all of our careers. Also, being able to capitalize on lucky situations is often predicated on having done great work."

> "The best way to maximize luck? Make great relationships + do your best work all the time."

## The Warning (Inside Straight Draw)
> Don't confuse lucky outcomes with good decisions.
> An inside straight draw wins ~16.5% of the time — some people hit it and think they're geniuses.
