# Consensus Hiring

[[Jordan Mazer]] is AGAINST consensus hiring as the decision mechanism.

## His Position
> "Hiring decisions are not and should not be consensus endeavors."
> Hiring managers are **ultimately accountable** for outcomes.

## Real Example
- He once hired a candidate who received **9 "no hire" votes** and 1 "strong hire" (his own)
- That candidate became one of the strongest performers in his 15-year career
- First interview was phone-only → no appearance bias

## What This Means For You
- He will defend unconventional candidates
- He doesn't need everyone to agree
- He respects conviction over consensus
