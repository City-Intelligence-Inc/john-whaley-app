# Startup Philosophy

[[Jordan Mazer]]'s case for working at startups.

## The Risk Reframe
> "Startup death ≠ career death."
> For high-skilled workers, lifeboats will be in the water if the ship sinks.

## The Upside
- More responsibility faster
- Higher learning density (learned more in year 1 at Scopely than years combined elsewhere)
- Equity participation can be life-changing (he owns a beach house)
- Better resume once it becomes a unicorn ("ex-[insert unicorn]")

## Paul Graham's Point (2008)
- Startups BENEFIT from economic turmoil while incumbents suffer
- Big companies cut headcount → less innovation
- Startups gain access to talent being cut

## His Sign-Off
> "Most startup jobs are not posted on LinkedIn. They are the next Cursor, Figma, OpenAI, Databricks..."
